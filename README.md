**Timing:** 10:00–18:00

![Herculaneum Ruins](https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/Special:FilePath/Herculaneum_03.JPG "Herculaneum ancient ruins")

- **Where:** coopculture.it
- **Cost:** €13/adult, reduced for under-18s
- **Why:** Less crowded than Pompeii, arguably better preserved (buried in mud, not ash)

# 📝 Practical Info

## What to Pack

**Essential:**
- ✅ Comfortable walking shoes (all 5 people) – **Pompeii requires sturdy shoes!**
- ✅ High SPF sunscreen (bring from home – expensive in Italy)
- ✅ Hats, sunglasses
- ✅ Light layers (April: 12–18°C mornings, 18–22°C afternoons)
- ✅ Reusable water bottle (refill at fountains – water is excellent)
- ✅ Phone chargers & power bank
- ✅ Small first-aid kit (plasters, paracetamol, antihistamine)
- ✅ Small day backpack for each person

## Eating Well on Budget

| | Cost | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **Lunch at Airbnb** | £3–5/person | Cook pasta, salads, leftovers – **BIGGEST SAVER** |
| **Dinner at Airbnb** | £5–7/person | Fresh pasta, grilled fish, local ingredients |
| **Street food** | €2–5/person | Pizza, frittatina, cuoppo, arancini, sfogliatella |
| **Restaurant (avoid)** | €15–20/person | Tourist traps = expensive |
| **Local trattoria** | €12–18/person | Authentic, reasonable, ONE meal as treat |

### Money-Saving Hacks
1. Shop at markets (Porta Nolana, Pignasecca, Capo)
2. Buy fresh, cook together (educational for kids!)
3. Picnics at sites
4. Street food for lunches
5. Avoid tourist restaurants

- **Rest day at Airbnb** – watch film, cook, recharge

## Safety in Naples

- Generally safe for tourists
- Avoid dark alleys alone at night
- Keep valuables (phone, wallet) secure in crowds
- **Pickpocketing exists** in metro & busy markets – stay aware
- Don't flash expensive cameras/phones
- Local people are friendly; ask for directions if lost
