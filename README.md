## Getting There & Around

### Airport to Airbnb (Arrival: 12:00 noon)

**Naples Capodichino Airport** → City centre
- **Best option:** Alibus shuttle bus (€5/person, ~20 mins)
  - Runs every 15–20 mins from Arrivals
  - Drops at Piazza Garibaldi / Naples Centrale station
  - Buy tickets at airport kiosks or on board
  - **Do NOT take taxis** (expensive, unpredictable traffic)
- **Alternative:** Metro (slower, requires connection, cheaper)

### Local Transport

**Essential:** Buy **5-day Campania Unica ticket** (€25/person = £30 total for family)
- Covers all metro lines (1, 2, 6), buses, trams, funiculars
- Covers Circumvesuviana train to Pompeii & Herculaneum
- Valid for 5 consecutive days
- Purchase at airport metro station or any metro kiosk on arrival
- Save receipt; validate before each journey

**Key Transport Routes:**
- **Metro Line 1:** North–south (Garibaldi to city centre)
- **Metro Line 2:** East–west (Garibaldi to coastal areas)
- **Circumvesuviana train:** Garibaldi Station (lower level) → Pompeii Scavi (35 mins) or Ercolano Scavi (20 mins)
- **Funiculars:** Vomero hill access from Via Toledo
- **Alibus:** Airport ↔ Piazza Garibaldi

## Day-by-Day Itinerary

### **DAY 1: Tuesday 7 April – Arrival & First Taste of Naples**

**12:00** Land at Naples Capodichino Airport (NAP)
- Take **Alibus shuttle** (€5/person) from Arrivals to Piazza Garibaldi / Naples Centrale
- Runs every 15–20 mins, takes ~20 mins
- Buy tickets at airport kiosks or on board

**13:00** Check in to Airbnb
- Coordinate with host for key collection
- Drop bags, freshen up
- Rest for 30 mins if jet-lagged

**13:45** Buy 5-day Campania Unica transport pass
- At metro station near Airbnb or Garibaldi
- €25/person (£30 total for family)
- Valid for all transport for entire stay

**14:00** First Naples Pizza!
- Head to **Via dei Tribunali** – "Pizza Alley"
- Try a classic **Margherita or Marinara** from a neighbourhood institution
  - L'Antica Pizzeria da Michele (famous, always busy)
  - Franco Pepe Pizzeria (excellent, less touristy)
- Eat it **folded in half, Naples-style**
- Budget: €5–8 per person
- **Tip:** Avoid places with picture menus; locals' choice is best

**15:00** Spaccanapoli Walk
- Ancient street cutting through city's heart (UNESCO site)
- See: **Church of Gesù Nuovo** (free, stunning baroque exterior)
- **Santa Chiara Cloister** (€6 entry, beautiful arcaded courtyard with majolica tiles)
- Wander through **Spanish Quarter** – spot Diego Maradona murals
- Browse **Via San Gregorio Armeno** – nativity scene crafts (year-round)
- Free to explore, just watch for scooters & street vendors!

**16:45** Piazza del Plebiscito & Waterfront
- Naples' grandest square – free
- See Royal Palace exterior
- Stroll **Lungomare** (coastal promenade) with bay views
- Pass **Castel dell'Ovo** – medieval castle on headland
  - Free to explore exterior and walk around
  - €1/person for interior (skip if tight on time)
- Photographs of Mt Vesuvius across the bay are spectacular at this hour

**17:45** Porta Nolana Fish Market
- One of Naples' best fish markets, near Garibaldi Station
- Pick up fresh seafood: whole fish, squid, clams, mussels
- Fishmongers grill samples on the spot – try before you buy
- Also source: fresh pasta, San Marzano tomatoes, basil, mozzarella
- Experience is an attraction in itself; kids love the bustle
- Budget: £20–25 for family dinner

**18:30** Return to Airbnb
- Cook a **Campanian seafood dinner**
  - Pasta alle vongole (clam pasta) – simple, authentic, delicious
  - Grilled fish with lemon & olive oil
  - Fresh salad with tomatoes
- Early night – big Pompeii day tomorrow

### **DAY 2: Wednesday 8 April – Pompeii Full Day**

**10:00** Depart Airbnb for Pompeii
- Walk to **Naples Garibaldi Station** (Centrale) or take metro depending on Airbnb location
- Validate transport pass

**10:30** Circumvesuviana Train to Pompeii
- Descend to lower level of Garibaldi Station for **Circumvesuviana line** (Napoli–Sorrento)
- **Cost:** €3.40/person (included in 5-day pass)
- **Duration:** ~35 mins
- **Get off at:** **Pompei Scavi – Villa dei Misteri** (not "Pompeii" proper station)
- Buy tickets at machines (cash or card) – validate before boarding

**11:00** Enter Pompeii Archaeological Site

⚠️ **CRITICAL: PRE-BOOK TIMED TICKETS ASAP**
- Book on **vivaticket.com** or **pompeiisites.org**
- In 2026, entry is **timed and strictly capped at 20,000/day**
- Morning slots (9:00–13:00) are most popular – **book 11:00 slot** for your family
- Tickets are **personal** (ID required at gate)
- **Cost (as of Jan 2026):**
    - **Basic Pompeii ticket:** €20/adult
    - **Pompeii+ ticket (recommended):** €25/adult (includes suburban villas & Villa of the Mysteries)
    - **Under-18s may be free or reduced** (check on booking)
- **Entry via:** Porta Marina entrance
- **Duration:** Allow 4–5 hours on site

**11:00–14:30** Explore Pompeii

**Key highlights (don't miss):**
- **The Forum** – heart of the city, temples, basilicas, public buildings
- **Amphitheatre** – one of the oldest in the world (kids find it impressive)
- **House of the Vettii** – stunning frescoes, private home of wealthy merchants
- **House of the Mysteries** – famous for erotic murals (age-appropriate in archaeological context)
- **Stabian Baths** – thermal pools, ancient heating systems
- **Garden of the Fugitives** – plaster casts of bodies caught in eruption (powerful, moving)
- **Bakery** – ovens still intact, ancient bread shapes visible
- **Thermopolium** – ancient fast-food bar with counter and storage jars

**Practicalities:**
- **Wear good shoes** – basalt cobbles are uneven, can twist ankles
- Excellent signage & maps at entrance; water fountains throughout
- Site café available if needed
- Shade is limited – bring sun protection

**14:30** Lunch Break
- **Option A:** Pack picnic from Airbnb (cheaper, ~£3/person)
  - Fresh bread, mozzarella, tomatoes, fruit
  - Eat in shaded spot on site
- **Option B:** Site café (basic but convenient, ~€8/person)
- Hydrate well – Pompeii is exhausting

**15:00** Leave Pompeii, Return to Naples
- Catch **Circumvesuviana back** to Garibaldi (trains every 30 mins)
- ~35 min journey
- Arrive Naples by **15:45**

**16:00** Porta Nolana Fish Market (Optional)
- If energy permits: stock up on fresh seafood for dinner
- Otherwise: grab pre-made food from supermarket instead
- Budget: £15–20 if shopping

**17:00** Return to Airbnb
- Freshen up, rest legs
- **Dinner:** Grilled or baked fresh fish, pasta, fresh salad
- Share highlights of the day over supper
- Early night – Pompeii is emotionally & physically exhausting

### **DAY 3: Thursday 9 April – Naples: Museums & Historic Centre**

**10:00** National Archaeological Museum (MANN)

⚠️ **PRE-BOOK TICKETS ONLINE**
- Home to world's finest Roman artefacts from Pompeii & Herculaneum
- **Tickets:** €12/adult, **under-18 free**
- **Duration:** 2.5–3 hours
- **Location:** Reachable via Metro Line 1 to "Museo" stop
- **Must-see exhibits:**
  - Mosaics (House of the Faun's "Alexander the Great battle")
  - Frescoes from Pompeii homes
  - Bronze sculptures
  - **Secret Cabinet (Gabinetto Segreto)** – erotic art collection (kids find it amusing & educational; context matters)
  - Plaster casts from Pompeii

**Book online to:**
- Skip queues (can be 1+ hours in peak season)
- Save ~€2/person
- Book on **coopculture.it** or museum website

**13:00** Return to Airbnb for Lunch
- Head back to accommodation
- Prepare & eat lunch together (€3–5/person)
  - Use ingredients from markets: fresh pasta, tomatoes, cheese, cold cuts
  - Leftovers from previous meals
  - Simple, family-friendly meal
- Rest & recharge for afternoon activities

**14:00** Via dei Tribunali & The Duomo

- Walk through Naples' **UNESCO historic centre**
- See street vendors, local life, narrow alleyways
- **Duomo (Cathedral of Naples)** – free entry, magnificent interior
  - Don't miss **Chapel of San Gennaro** – glittering gold, baroque splendour
  - San Gennaro is Naples' patron saint; his reliquary here
- **Via San Gregorio Armeno** – famous for nativity scene crafts (year-round)
  - Browse craftspeople at work
  - Buy if interested (small figurines €5–20)

**15:15** Napoli Sotterranea (Underground Naples)

Optional but highly recommended:
- Descend into 2,400-year-old network of Greek aqueducts & WWII shelters
- **Guided tour:** ~1 hour, fascinating for all ages
- **Cost:** €12/adult, €5–8/children
- **Book:** On arrival or online (search "Napoli Sotterranea")
- Ask guide to explain both Greek & WWII history; kids find the shelters powerful

**16:30** Toledo Metro Station & Spanish Quarter

- **Toledo station** – voted Europe's most beautiful metro station
  - Marvel at mosaic interiors, artistic tiles
  - Free to visit; hop on Line 1 from wherever you are
  - Spend 10 mins admiring, take photos
- Walk up into **Spanish Quarter** – labyrinthine narrow streets
  - Spot **Diego Maradona murals** (local hero)
  - See local life: laundry lines, street vendors, locals playing cards
  - Free to explore; just be aware of pickpockets in crowds

**17:15** Supermarket Shop for Dinner

- Pick up Campanian specialities:
  - Fresh pasta (tagliatelle, spaghetti, pappardelle)
  - San Marzano tomatoes (canned is fine)
  - **Mozzarella di bufala** (local treasure, ~€4)
  - Fresh basil
  - Local wine (red or white, €5–10)
  - Limoncello (digestif, €8–12)
- Budget: £20–25 for dinner + breakfast items

**18:30** Return to Airbnb

- Cook a **true Neapolitan dinner:**
  - **Spaghetti al pomodoro** (tomatoes, garlic, basil, olive oil) – simple perfection
  - OR **Pasta e fagioli** (pasta & beans, hearty, family-friendly)
  - Fresh salad
  - Mozzarella di bufala on the side
- Adults: glass of local wine
- Finish with limoncello (digestif) or fruit

### **DAY 4: Friday 10 April – Herculaneum & Coastal Sites**

**10:00** Depart for Herculaneum

- Head to **Naples Garibaldi Station** (Centrale)
- Validate 5-day transport pass

**10:20** Circumvesuviana Train to Ercolano Scavi

- Same train as Pompeii, but **get off earlier**
- **Cost:** €2.20/person (included in 5-day pass)
- **Duration:** Only ~20 mins from Garibaldi
- **Get off at:** **Ercolano Scavi** stop
- Walk downhill ~10 mins to site entrance

**10:30** Herculaneum Archaeological Park

⚠️ **PRE-BOOK TICKETS**
- Book on **coopculture.it**
- **Cost:** €13/adult, reduced for under-18s
- **Duration:** 2.5–3 hours
- Far less crowded than Pompeii; arguably better preserved
- City was buried in mud (not ash), preserving wooden furniture, food, textiles – unique

**Must-see highlights:**
- **The Boathouses** – 300 skeletons visible, haunting & moving (explain context to children)
- **House of Neptune & Amphitrite** – beautiful mosaic, upper-floor rooms intact
- **Thermopolium** – ancient fast-food bar with counter, storage jars, carbonized food
- **Upper-floor buildings** – rare to see multiple storeys preserved; shows how people lived
- **Casa dell'Atrio a Mosaico** – mosaic-floored home

**Practical:**
- Smaller site than Pompeii – easier to navigate for younger kids
- Fewer crowds = more peaceful experience
- Shade available in some areas; bring sun protection

**13:30** Picnic Lunch in Ercolano

- **Option A:** Bring lunch from Airbnb (cheese, bread, fruit, drinks – £3/person)
  - Sit on site's viewing terrace
  - Stunning views of excavations below sea level
- **Option B:** Grab food from local bar in Ercolano town (very affordable, €3–5/person)

**14:30** Return to Naples

- Circumvesuviana back to Garibaldi
- Arrive by ~15:00
- This leaves a free afternoon – good for rest or optional sites

**15:00** Castel Nuovo (Maschio Angioino)

Optional but impressive:
- Grand medieval castle right on the port
- **Entry:** €6 (Civic Museum inside)
- **Free:** Walk ramparts, view exterior, photograph triumphal arch
- Great views of Naples harbour
- Photogenic & historically significant

**16:15** Lungomare Stroll & Castel dell'Ovo

- Walk the seafront promenade one last time
- **Castel dell'Ovo** – Naples' oldest castle on its own little headland
  - Free entry
  - Check opening status on arrival (may still be under renovation)
  - Even if closed, walk around exterior & harbour at **Borgo Marinari**
  - Beautiful spot; good for sunset photos

**17:00** Head Home for Seafood Dinner

- If energy permits: pick up fresh catch at fishmonger en route (£10–15)
- Otherwise: grab pre-prepared food from supermarket

**18:00** Final Dinner (Campanian Seafood)

- Classic **baked fish with cherry tomatoes & olives**
- Fresh pasta with seafood
- Local wine
- Relax – you've earned it

  - Perfect for picking up ingredients for dinner & souvenirs

## Practical Notes

### Transport & Navigation

- **Metro validation:** Stamp your ticket in the machine before boarding
- **Late-night transport:** Metro runs until ~23:00; after that use buses
- **Beware pickpockets:** In crowded metro & markets – keep valuables secure
- **Apps to download:**
  - Google Maps (offline mode)
  - Citymapper (Naples metro info)
  - Moovit (real-time transport)

### Weather (Early April in Naples)

- **Temperature:** 12–18°C mornings, 18–22°C afternoons
- **Rainfall:** Occasional showers – bring light rain jacket
- **Sun:** Strong even in April; sunscreen essential

### Language Tips

- **Learn these phrases:**
  - "Grazie" (thank you)
  - "Per favore" (please)
  - "Ciao" (hello/goodbye)
  - "Quanto costa?" (how much?)
  - "Due caffè, per favore" (two coffees, please)
- **Most younger Italians speak English**; hotels & major sites have English speakers
- **Smile & be polite:** Italians are warm if you show respect

### Restaurants vs. Airbnb Cooking

- **Tourist restaurants:** €15–20/person (expensive)
- **Local osterias:** €12–15/person (better value, still pricey)
- **Street food:** €2–5/person (excellent quality, cheap)
- **Airbnb cooking:** £3–5/person (best value, more control, fun family activity)

### Safety in Naples

- Naples has a reputation but is generally safe for tourists
- Avoid dark alleys alone at night
- Keep valuables (phone, wallet) secure in crowds
- Pickpocketing exists in metro & busy markets – stay aware
- Don't flash expensive cameras/phones
- Local people are friendly; ask for directions if lost
